Best Country to Create a Startup

Why Create a Startup Outside of Your Home Country

Starting a startup in a foreign country can offer numerous advantages. These may include access to larger or more receptive markets, favorable regulatory environments, better funding opportunities, exposure to new ideas and technologies, and potential tax benefits. Additionally, some countries offer special visas or incentives to attract foreign entrepreneurs, fostering an ecosystem that supports innovation and growth.

Success Stories of Startups Created on Entrepreneur Visas

  • TransferWise (now Wise): Founded by Estonian entrepreneurs Kristo Käärmann and Taavet Hinrikus in the UK, TransferWise revolutionized international money transfers. They utilized the UK's Tier 1 Entrepreneur Visa to build a fintech unicorn offering low-cost, transparent currency exchange services.
  • BlaBlaCar: French entrepreneur Frédéric Mazzella started BlaBlaCar, a long-distance carpooling platform, expanding across Europe. Leveraging supportive EU regulations, the company grew rapidly, fostering shared economy principles.
  • Zoho Corporation: Founded by Sridhar Vembu, an Indian entrepreneur, Zoho set up significant operations in the USA under business visas. Zoho offers a suite of online productivity tools and SaaS applications, competing globally with major tech companies.
  • Rovio Entertainment: The Finnish company behind "Angry Birds" was founded by entrepreneurs who benefited from Finland's supportive startup ecosystem and entrepreneur residence permits, allowing them to create a globally successful gaming franchise.
  • Rocket Lab: Founded by New Zealander Peter Beck, Rocket Lab moved significant operations to the USA, taking advantage of investor support and the H-1B visa program. Rocket Lab has become a key player in the small satellite launch industry.

Top Startup Ecosystems Around the World

The global startup ecosystem map highlights cities and regions renowned for fostering innovation and entrepreneurship. These ecosystems attract entrepreneurs worldwide seeking opportunities to scale their startups in dynamic markets.

Ease of Doing Business Index by Country

Countries ranked at the top of the Ease of Doing Business Index offer regulatory environments conducive to starting and operating a business. High-ranking countries often have streamlined processes for business registration, favorable tax regimes, strong legal protections, and efficient resolution of insolvency. Entrepreneurs may consider these countries for their startups due to the reduced bureaucratic hurdles and supportive infrastructures.

Viewing the Ease of Doing Business Index alphabetically allows for easy lookup of specific countries and a comparison of their relative standings. This perspective helps entrepreneurs identify potential countries of interest and understand where they stand in terms of business friendliness compared to others.

Economic Indicators of Countries

This bubble chart visualizes countries based on Purchasing Power Parity (PPP), PPP per capita, population, and region over time. The size and position of the bubbles represent economic size and wealth, while the color indicates the region. Filtering by region allows entrepreneurs to focus on specific areas of interest. Understanding these economic indicators is crucial when considering market potential, consumer purchasing power, and overall economic health of a country.

Income Levels and Service Sector Contribution

The choropleth map of median income by country provides insight into the average income levels of populations worldwide. Higher median incomes can indicate greater consumer spending power, which may be attractive for startups targeting affluent markets. Entrepreneurs can use this information to assess potential markets for their products or services.

The service sector percentage map illustrates the proportion of a country's economy derived from services. A higher percentage suggests a mature economy with opportunities in sectors like technology, finance, and professional services. Startups in these fields may find more developed infrastructure and customer bases in such countries.
